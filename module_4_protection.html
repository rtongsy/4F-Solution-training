4F Solution — Lesson 1: Why protection comes second

You cannot grow what you cannot protect

Cash flow tells you what is available. Protection ensures it stays available. A single unplanned event can erase everything built across the other three Fundamentals.

The logic of the sequence
Cash flow defines the foundation. Protection ensures nothing eliminates it before the plan delivers on it. Investments & Wealth and Tax Efficiency build on a base that must be stable first.
What protection actually is
Protection is not insurance. Insurance is one of the tools. Protection is the conversation about what could go wrong — and what happens to the client's plan, their family, and their goals if it does.
The connection to values
Every protection gap is a threat to something the client told you they care about. A family security value with no life insurance is a contradiction. An independence value with no disability coverage is a vulnerability. Surface the connection — clearly, and without fear tactics.
4F Solution — Lesson 2: The four risks

Four events. Any one of them can erase the plan.

Each one stops or reduces income while life continues — and the plan assumes income keeps coming.

Risk 1
Death
Income stops permanently. Everything that depended on it is immediately at risk.
Risk 2
Disability
Income stops but expenses continue — often increase. More likely than premature death.
Risk 3
Critical Illness
Surviving a serious diagnosis creates a financial crisis the medical system does not solve.
Risk 4
Income Loss
Job loss or business disruption. No insurance product covers this — cash reserves only.
What all four have in common
Each disrupts income while life — and its costs — continues. Protection planning is the answer to what happens when income stops, for whatever reason.
4F Solution — Lesson 3: Life insurance

Life insurance — protecting what depends on you

Not about death. About what the client's income currently makes possible — and what happens to all of that when it stops permanently.

What it does
Replaces income, pays off debt, and funds future goals when the client is no longer there to do it. Term covers a defined period. Permanent covers a lifetime and can build cash value. The need drives the choice.
Discovery question
"If you were no longer here tomorrow — what would you want to make sure was taken care of, and for how long?"
What to listen for: "I have coverage through work" — usually far less than they think, and none that follows them if they leave. An opening, not an objection.
No recommendations during discovery
The professional surfaces the gap and connects it to values. What closes it — which product, what coverage amount — comes at the solution delivery meeting.
4F Solution — Lesson 4: Disability

Disability — the most overlooked risk in financial planning

Most clients plan for death. Almost none plan for surviving but being unable to work. Disability is more likely than premature death — and financially more complex.

Why it is more complex
When someone dies, expenses largely go with them. When someone becomes disabled, expenses continue — often increase — while income disappears. The gap is larger, lasts longer, and is harder to plan for after the fact.
Enough to have the conversation
Disability insurance replaces 60–70% of income when the client cannot work. Group coverage is partial and not portable. Self-employed and incorporated clients typically have no employer coverage at all.
Discovery question
"If you could not work for six months — how long could your household maintain its current lifestyle? And do you have coverage that would kick in?"
What to listen for: "I have sick days" or "my employer covers me" — usually 30–90 days, then nothing. Both need an honest conversation about the gap.
4F Solution — Lesson 5: Critical illness

Critical illness — the financial cost of surviving

Surviving a serious diagnosis is increasingly common. But recovery takes months — and the financial impact is often larger than the medical event itself.

What most clients do not realize
During recovery, income may stop, treatment costs emerge, and caregiving demands increase — all at once. The medical system treats the illness. It does not solve the financial crisis that comes with it.
Enough to have the conversation
Critical illness pays a tax-free lump sum on diagnosis of a covered condition — regardless of whether the client can work. It is not income replacement. It is financial flexibility at the moment options are most limited.
Discovery question
"If you were diagnosed with something serious tomorrow — would you want your family focused on supporting you, or managing the financial pressure that comes with it?"
What to listen for: "I would use my savings" — depletes the asset base and sets back every other goal. "We would manage" — they have not run the numbers. Both are openings.
4F Solution — Lesson 6: Income loss

Income loss — the risk with no insurance product

Job loss and business disruption can derail a plan just as completely as any other risk — and they are far more common. Cash reserves are the only tool.

Why this conversation rarely happens
There is nothing to sell — no product, no premium. So most professionals skip it. The professional who raises it builds trust. The one who skips it leaves a visible gap in the client's picture.
The cash flow connection
The surplus identified in the cash flow discovery is what funds the reserve that protects against income loss. This is the clearest example of how the Fundamentals build on each other.
Discovery question
"If your income stopped tomorrow — how many months could your household sustain its current lifestyle without any changes?"
Government benefits are a factor for some — not a plan. Most are partial, time-limited, and unavailable to self-employed or incorporated individuals.
4F Solution — Lesson 7: Living documents

Will, Power of Attorney, and Health directive — the documents most clients do not have

Insurance addresses the financial consequences of a risk event. Living documents address who makes the decisions. Without both, protection is incomplete.

Will — directing what you leave behind
Without a Will, the estate follows the jurisdiction's default rules — which may not reflect the client's wishes. Names the executor, directs assets, and for clients with children, names a guardian. Most clients either don't have one or haven't reviewed it since a major life event.
Power of Attorney — who manages your affairs
Without a POA, no one is legally authorized to manage the client's financial affairs if they become incapacitated. This gap creates enormous stress for families at the worst possible moment.
Health directive — who makes medical decisions
Specifies the client's medical wishes and names someone to act on them. Without one, critical decisions are left to whoever is present — or to the legal system.
Discovery question
"Do you have a current Will, a Power of Attorney, and a Health directive in place? And when were they last reviewed?"
The professional surfaces the gap. Recommendations go in the complete plan.
4F Solution — Lesson 8: Connecting gaps to values

Every protection gap is a threat to something the client told you they care about

A gap without a values connection is a product pitch. Connected to what the client told you matters — it is financial advice.

Family security — no disability coverage
"You told me your family's security is what matters most. Right now, if you became unable to work, your income stops and their lifestyle is immediately at risk. That is the gap we need to close."
Independence — no income reserve
"You told me financial independence is the goal. Right now, one disruption to your income puts everything you have built at risk. The plan needs a buffer before it can build toward independence."
Legacy — no permanent coverage
"You told me you want to leave something behind for your children. Right now, there is nothing in place to ensure that happens regardless of when you die. That is a gap the complete plan needs to address."
No recommendations during discovery
Surface the gap. Connect it to values. What closes it comes at the solution delivery meeting — after the full picture across all four Fundamentals is built.
